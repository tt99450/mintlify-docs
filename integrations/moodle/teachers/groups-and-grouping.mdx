title: "Groups and grouping"

---

Groups work much the same way in BigBlueButton as they do in the rest of Moodle, but BigBlueButton adds one important behavior: there can be a unique room per group.

That means the **Join** button can send a user to:

- the room for the group they belong to
- a room they choose from a group selector
- a general room in some teacher-facing cases

## Group options

The main group mode options are:

- **Separate groups**
- **Visible groups**

## Separate groups

This is the most restrictive option.

In many cases:

- students only see the group they belong to
- users who belong to multiple allowed groups may choose from a limited list
- educators can choose from a wider set of rooms

Teachers may need to move between rooms to meet with students who have joined each group room.

## Visible groups

This is more permissive.

Users can choose the room they want to join from a list that shows all groups available in the course, subject to site and role configuration.

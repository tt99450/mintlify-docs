## A presenter in BigBlueButton can use screen sharing to display content from their local desktop/laptop or Chromebook in Chrome and Firefox browsers.

**Screen sharing is currently not supported on mobile devices. To share a screen, the presenter must use a desktop, laptop, or Chromebook.**

Use screen sharing to show viewers dynamic content or content that cannot be uploaded into BigBlueButton as a static PDF; you can share your entire screen or an application window from your local desktop or laptop device.

If the session is being recorded, screen sharing is included in the recording and will be viewable in the playback.

**Safari, Internet Explorer, and Edge browsers currently do not support WebRTC-enabled screen share.**

By default, the first **Moderator** to enter the BigBlueButton session is allocated the presenter role capabilities; if you are a viewer, you will need to request presenter status from the moderator.

## Chrome

Be sure to open any applications or additional browser windows you wish to share before engaging the screen sharing in BigBlueButton.

<Frame>
  ![Chrome](/images/chrome.png)
</Frame>

If you are using Chrome browser, you will be prompted to choose either your **Chrome Tab** **\[1\]** or an application **Window** **\[2\]** or **Entire Screen** **\[3\]**.

Once you have configured the screen share to your preference, select **Share** **\[4\]** to begin sharing with attendees.

**IMPORTANT NOTES:**

- When sharing your **Entire Screen** **\[1\]**, users will see your screen even when navigating to another browser or application window. This setting is recommended if you plan to use several different applications and do not wish to reconfigure the screen share with each change.
- When sharing an application **Window** **\[2\]**, users will only see the application selected; if you navigate outside the chosen application, the users will **not** see these actions. This setting is recommended if you plan to use the same application; sharing an application window also requires less bandwidth than sharing your entire screen.
- Sharing your screen with **Chrome Tab** **\[3\]** on the latest version of Chrome will allow you to quickly switch between different tabs by going to the tab to share and clicking “**Share this tab instead**”.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot 2026 03 24 At 1 50 31 PM](/images/Screenshot2026-03-24at1.50.31PM.png)
</Frame>

- **Error 1136: Permission to capture the screen needs to be granted.** If your Chrome is open and it's halfway through updating, then screen share will not be recognized and you might get this error. To overcome this, restart Chrome (and let it finish updating) and then you will be able to screen share again.

## Firefox

Be sure to open any applications or additional browser windows you wish to share before engaging the screen sharing in BigBlueButton.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot 2026 03 24 At 1 44 24 PM](/images/Screenshot2026-03-24at1.44.24PM.png)
</Frame>

If you are using Firefox browser, you will be asked for screen sharing permissions. Be sure to select **Allow**.

If you do not see the window you would like to share, it may not be open. Be sure to open the application or window you intend to share, then restart the screen sharing. You should see the window in the list of available options.

## What users see

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot 2026 03 24 At 1 38 48 PM](/images/Screenshot2026-03-24at1.38.48PM.png)
</Frame>

Users will see the screen share displayed in the presentation area of the BigBlueButton interface. It no longer loads into a separate window.

![Screen\_Shot\_2019-05-01\_at\_4.01.39\_PM.png](/images/360027566512.png)

When the presenter is in BigBlueButton, screen sharing will duplicate to create a mirrored broadcast of the screen. Don’t worry -- this is not what viewers see when in another tab or an application window.

## A presenter in BigBlueButton can control and annotate content uploaded into the presentation area or the whiteboard

The presenter role enables you to:

1. [Control the slide navigation](#slide-navigation)
2. [Use the slide/whiteboard annotations](/help/releases/how-do-i-use-the-multi-user-whiteboard)
3. [Enable the multiuser whiteboard](/help/releases/how-do-i-use-the-multi-user-whiteboard) for use by attendees

By default, the first moderator to enter the BigBlueButton session is allocated the presenter role capabilities. If you are a viewer, you will need to request presenter status from the moderator.

<Frame>
  ![Screenshot 2026 03 16 At 4 34 45 PM](/images/Screenshot2026-03-16at4.34.45PM.png)
</Frame>

When a user has the presenter role, the **presenter icon** will appear over their avatar in the users list.

## Slide navigation

Once you have uploaded content into the BigBlueButton presentation area, you can control the navigation and visual orientation of the content displayed.

<Frame>
  ![Slidesbar](/images/slidesbar-1.png)
</Frame>

The presenter's slide/content control panel is located under the presentation display and above your media control icons.

<Frame>
  ![Slidesbaroptions](/images/slidesbaroptions.png)
</Frame>

To navigate through your content, use the **Slide Navigator** **[1]** located in the presenter control panel. Advance using the **forward "\>"** navigation arrows, and return using the **backward "\<"** navigation arrows. Skip to a specific slide by selecting the slide number from the **dropdown**.

Select the **multi-user whiteboard button [2]** to activate whiteboard use for all users.

<Frame>
  ![Whiteboardslidesbar](/images/whiteboardslidesbar.png)
</Frame>

A prompt will appear at the upper-right corner saying it's available, and the multi-user whiteboard icon will be highlighted in the bar. 

The other options are **Zoom In (+) or Out (-)** **[3]** or use **Fit-to-Width** **[4]**.

When uploading a portrait document, use Fit-to-Width to make the text larger. You can then click and drag with the hand tool to move up/down the document.

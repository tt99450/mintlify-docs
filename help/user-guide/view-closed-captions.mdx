---
title: "View closed captions"
description: "Anyone in a session can view closed captions, generated either automatically by the browser or by a live stenographer."
---

Anyone in a BigBlueButton session can view closed captions. Captions can come from three sources: [automatic captions from your microphone](#automatic-captions-from-your-microphone), [automatic captions from your audio output](#automatic-captions-from-your-audio-output), or [a live stenographer](#stenographer-legacy).

## Automatic captions from your microphone

BigBlueButton supports per-user captioning using the browser's [WebSpeech API](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/SpeechRecognition).

When this feature is enabled, select your spoken language from the dropdown when joining audio to have your voice transcribed.

<Frame>
  ![Language drop-down selector in the join audio dialog for choosing a transcription language](/images/20536346091405.png)
</Frame>

Once you start speaking, a **CC** icon appears next to your name in the "who is talking" indicator.

<Frame>
  ![mceclip4.png](/images/19186661081741.png)
</Frame>

A **CC** button also appears below the presentation area.

<Frame>
  ![CC button displayed below the presentation area for viewing transcriptions](/images/20536301856525.png)
</Frame>

To view the transcriptions for any user with a **CC** indicator, select the **CC** button. You do not need your own audio to be transcribed to view captions for others — when at least one user has their audio transcribed, the **CC** button appears for everyone.

Desktop users can change the detected language during the session by selecting the up arrow on the **CC** button and choosing a different language — for example, if you switch to speaking Spanish for the rest of the session.

<Frame>
  ![Up arrow on the CC button opening the language selection menu](/images/20536346105229.png)
</Frame>

<Note>
  On mobile devices, the **CC** button is in the three-dot menu in the top-right corner.

  ![mceclip7.png](/images/19186676147981.png)

  Automatic transcription is only available on browsers that support SpeechRecognition (Chrome, Edge, Safari). Other browsers display a warning at the bottom of the join audio dialog.

  ![mceclip9.png](/images/19186676150669.png)

  Per-user captioning is disabled by default on Premium Tier accounts. To enable it, contact Blindside Networks support.
</Note>

## Automatic captions from your audio output

You can configure Chrome to caption all spoken audio coming through your speaker. See Google's [Live Caption blog post](https://blog.google/products/chrome/live-caption-chrome/#:~:text=To%20turn%20on%20Live%20Caption,be%20coming%20soon%20to%20ChromeOS.) for setup instructions.

These captions are generated by the browser, not BigBlueButton, and do not appear in recordings.

## Stenographer (legacy)

BigBlueButton also supports a live, human stenographer who creates captions in real time. Closed captions only appear when the stenographer has started transcribing — until then, no caption option is available.

### View stenographer captions

<Frame>
  ![Closed Caption icon in the lower-left corner of the presentation area](/images/360034179452.png)
</Frame>

To start viewing captions, select the **Closed Caption** icon in the lower-left corner of the presentation area.

<Frame>
  ![Closed caption preferences dialog](/images/360034168051.png)
</Frame>

Choose your **Preferred Language** **[1]**, then adjust the **Text Colour** **[2]**, **Background Colour** **[3]**, **Font Style** **[4]**, and **Font Size** **[5]**.

Select **Start** **[6]** to begin viewing captions in real time.

Captions appear in the presentation area as the stenographer transcribes:

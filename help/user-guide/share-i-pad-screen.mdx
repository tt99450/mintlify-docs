---
title: "Share iPad screen"
description: "Share an iPad screen in a BigBlueButton session by mirroring the iPad to a Mac and screen-sharing the QuickTime window."
---

Safari on iPad cannot share its screen directly into BigBlueButton. To share an iPad screen, mirror the iPad to a Mac using QuickTime Player, then screen-share the QuickTime window from the Mac in your BigBlueButton session.

The result is that other users in the session see your iPad screen as a live view.

## Connect the iPad to your Mac

Connect your iPad to your Mac with a USB-C cable. When the iPad prompts you, select **Allow**.

<Frame>
  ![iPad Allow connection prompt](/images/image-30.png)
</Frame>

To verify the connection, open **Finder**. Your iPad appears under **Locations** — for example, **iPad Pro (2)** in the screenshot below.

<Frame>
  ![Finder showing connected iPad in Locations](/images/image-32.png)
</Frame>

## Mirror the iPad with QuickTime Player

Open **QuickTime Player** and select **New Movie Recording**.

<Frame>
  ![QuickTime Player New Movie Recording menu](/images/image-31.png)
</Frame>

Select the **arrow** next to the red **Record** button to open the source menu.

<Frame>
  ![QuickTime source menu next to the Record button](/images/image-39.png)
</Frame>

In the source menu, choose your iPad by name (for example, **iPad Pro (2)**).

<Frame>
  ![QuickTime source menu with iPad selected](/images/image-33.png)
</Frame>

You do not need to actually record. The goal is to display your iPad screen on the Mac so it can be screen-shared.

## Screen-share the QuickTime window

In BigBlueButton, select **Share screen** in the media bar. Choose **Window**, then **Movie Recording**, then **Share**.

<Frame>
  ![Browser screen-share dialog with Movie Recording window selected](/images/image-36.png)
</Frame>

BigBlueButton shares the iPad screen to all users in the session. Anything you do on the iPad is visible to everyone live.

<Frame>
  ![iPad screen shared in a BigBlueButton session](/images/image-37.png)
</Frame>

---
title: "How to disable IPv6"
description: "Disable IPv6 on Windows, macOS, or your Spectrum router to stop your home connection from dropping BigBlueButton sessions."
---

If your connection to BigBlueButton repeatedly reconnects from home, your router may be inadvertently closing the connection. This is common on Spectrum networks in the US.

To resolve this, disable IPv6 on your computer or router using the steps below.

## Disable IPv6 on Windows 10

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the network menu">
    Right-click the **Network ![Windows 10 Network Icon](/images/4r4aezh4fi2hu3r1ummexkzcvq8ht7n7.png "Windows 10 Network Icon") / Wi-Fi ![Windows 10 Network Icon](/images/pyzl69tqpolp0rn9k36jhwo3ee0g9zuu.png "Windows 10 Network Icon")** icon in the bottom-right corner of your screen. A pop-up menu appears.

    ![Open Network Settings](/images/bbex32h65fl5fy97btps71ndozy5kmhc.png "Open Network Settings")
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Network and Sharing Center">
    Select **Open Network and Sharing Center**. The Settings dialog appears.

    ![Network Sharing Center](/images/iff9tqhfex8rwb1ubkd3y0mxrcqvokcr.png "Network Sharing Center")
  </Step>
  <Step title="Change adapter options">
    Select **Change adapter options**. A list of network adapters appears. Locate the active adapter — either **Ethernet** or **Wi-Fi**.

    ![Network Adapters](/images/5r6rgh3kkdietg84hx1n8vir509sfey1.png "Network Adapters")
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open adapter properties">
    Right-click the active network adapter and select **Properties**.

    ![Adapter Properties](/images/xosvx7peb02jlb9f1cy6ulqq107o7d3g.png "Adapter Properties")
  </Step>
  <Step title="Disable IPv6">
    In the **Properties** window, scroll to **Internet Protocol Version 6 (TCP/IPv6)**.

    ![Properties Window](/images/8e4itodb1912szkqdjfl1818r1gmel2l.png "Properties Window")

    Uncheck the **Internet Protocol Version 6 (TCP/IPv6)** checkbox, select **OK** to save the changes, and restart your computer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Disable IPv6 on macOS

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Network preferences">
    Open **System Preferences** and select the **Network** icon.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your network">
    Select the network you are using — usually labeled **Wi-Fi**.

    ![Network preferences](/images/mceclip0_2.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open advanced settings">
    Select **Advanced…**.

    ![Advanced button](/images/mceclip1_3.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the TCP/IP tab">
    Select the **TCP/IP** tab.

    ![TCP/IP tab](/images/mceclip3.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set IPv6 to link-local only">
    Open the **Configure IPv6** drop-down menu and set it to **Link-local only**.

    ![Configure IPv6 menu](/images/mceclip4.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and restart">
    Select **OK**, then restart your Mac.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Disable IPv6 on your Spectrum router

If you have the **rac2v1k** Spectrum router, you can disable IPv6 from the router's web-based interface.

To sign in, find the URL and password on the back of the router — typically [http://192.168.1.1](http://192.168.1.1/) with `admin`/`admin`.

Once signed in, select **Advanced** at the top, select **IPv6** on the left, and under **IPv6 LAN settings** choose **Disable**.

![Disable IPv6 on Spectrum router](/images/mceclip0_3.png)

Select **Apply**. After a few moments, the router confirms the update is complete. Try joining the BigBlueButton session again.

---
title: "Cannot join audio on macOS after allowing browser permissions"
description: "Re-enable microphone access for your browser in macOS System Preferences so you can join audio in a BigBlueButton session."
---

Anyone joining a BigBlueButton session on macOS needs to grant the browser permission to use the microphone. If you previously denied that permission, macOS blocks audio even after the in-session prompts succeed.

## Grant microphone permission at the system prompt

![macOS microphone permission prompt](/images/Screen_Shot_2018-10-31_at_4.40.54_PM.png)

The first time you join a session after a system update, macOS prompts you to allow the browser to access your microphone.

To grant access, select **OK**.

If you selected **Don't Allow**, or you suspect you did, macOS blocks your microphone even after you allow access in the browser. Re-enable it in **System Preferences**.

## Re-enable microphone access for your browser

![macOS Apple menu](/images/Screen_Shot_2018-10-31_at_4.37.27_PM.png)

To open system settings, select the Apple menu and choose **System Preferences**.

![System Preferences window](/images/Screen_Shot_2018-10-31_at_4.38.59_PM.png)

To open privacy settings, select **Security & Privacy**.

![Microphone privacy settings with privacy, microphone, and application checkbox callouts](/images/Screen_Shot_2018-11-01_at_5.09.35_PM.png)

To find the microphone list, select the **Privacy** **[1]** tab, then select **Microphone** **[2]** from the sidebar.

A list of applications that have requested microphone access appears.

To allow your browser to use the microphone, check the box next to the application **[3]**.

<Note>
Restart the browser before rejoining the session so the new permission takes effect.
</Note>

You can now share your microphone in BigBlueButton.
